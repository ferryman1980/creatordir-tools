Best AI Photo Colorization & Restoration Tools 2026

Black-and-white photographs hold memories, but color brings them to life. AI-powered colorization and restoration tools have improved dramatically, making it possible to add realistic color to vintage photos, repair damaged images, and enhance faded details. In 2026, these tools are accessible to anyone — no Photoshop skills required. Here are our top picks after extensive testing.

Palette.fm

Palette.fm is our top recommendation for AI colorization. It allows you to colorize photos instantly and then adjust the color palette with text descriptions — for example, "warm vintage tones" or "cold metallic look." The base tier is free, and the Pro plan ($9/month) unlocks higher resolutions and batch processing. The color accuracy is the best we have seen.

Colorize.cc

Colorize.cc is a straightforward, no-frills tool that delivers excellent results on portraits and landscapes. It uses a deep-learning model trained on millions of color images. The free version adds a small watermark, while the paid version ($0.99 per image or $10/month unlimited) removes it. It also includes automatic scratch and dust removal.

MyHeritage Photo Enhancer

MyHeritage gained fame for its AI photo animation feature, but its colorization and restoration tools are equally impressive. It excels at old family photos, accurately guessing skin tones and period-appropriate clothing colors. The tool is free for the first 10 photos, with a subscription required after that.

Remini

Remini is best known for face enhancement, but its colorization module is surprisingly good. It works particularly well on 1950s–1970s color-faded photos, restoring original hues rather than inventing new ones from scratch. Pricing starts at $9.99/month.

Hotpot AI Restore

Hotpot's AI restoration tool combines face enhancement, scratch removal, colorization, and upscaling in one pipeline. It is web-based and requires no installation. The free tier is generous (up to 10 images per day), making it ideal for casual users.

Pro Tips for Best Results

Start with the highest resolution scan you can get. AI colorization works best when it has more pixel data to work with. For damaged photos, use a restoration tool first to fix cracks and tears, then apply colorization. Most tools work best on portraits; landscapes and cityscapes may require palette adjustments.
