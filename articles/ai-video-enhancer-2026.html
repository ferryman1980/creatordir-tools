Best AI Video Enhancer & Upscaling Tools 2026

Low-resolution footage used to mean unusable footage. That is no longer true. AI-powered video enhancers can upscale 480p to 4K, remove noise, sharpen details, and even restore old family videos. In 2026, the technology has matured to the point where the results are often indistinguishable from native high-resolution recording. Here are the top tools we tested.

Topaz Video AI

Topaz remains the gold standard for professional video upscaling. It offers multiple models optimized for different content types — animation, live-action, and low-light footage. The latest version supports up to 8K output with temporal consistency that eliminates flickering. It is desktop-only (Windows & macOS) and requires a powerful GPU, but the quality is unmatched. A single license costs $299, and there is no subscription.

AVCLabs Video Enhancer AI

AVCLabs is the best alternative to Topaz, offering similar quality at a lower price point ($19.95/month or $79.95 lifetime). It excels at face detail reconstruction and supports batch processing. The interface is more beginner-friendly, making it a great choice for creators who do not want to dive into model selection.

HitPaw Video Enhancer

HitPaw offers a compelling combination of quality and speed. Its proprietary "Face Model" does an excellent job reconstructing facial details in talking-head videos. The software also includes colorization and frame interpolation. Pricing starts at $35.99/month or $75.99 for a perpetual license.

CapCut (Free Tier)

Believe it or not, CapCut's built-in video enhance feature is surprisingly capable for a free tool. While it cannot match Topaz or AVCLabs in extreme upscales (4x and beyond), it is perfect for quick 2x enhancements on social media clips. It works entirely in the browser and on mobile.

Clipchamp (Microsoft)

Clipchamp, now bundled with Windows 11, includes AI-powered auto-enhance features. It is not as powerful as dedicated tools, but it is free and already on your computer. Great for quick touch-ups before uploading to YouTube or TikTok.

Which Tool Should You Choose?

For professional filmmakers and restoration work: Topaz Video AI is the clear winner. For everyday creators on a budget: AVCLabs offers the best value. For quick social media clips: CapCut or Clipchamp will suffice. Most tools offer free trials, so test them on your own footage before committing.
